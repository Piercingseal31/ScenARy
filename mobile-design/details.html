The Basilica Minore del Santo Niño is the oldest Roman Catholic church in the country. It was founded in 1565 by Spanish explorers, led by Miguel López de Legazpi, who discovered an image of the Child Jesus (Santo Niño) believed to have been given by Ferdinand Magellan to Queen Juana in 1521.

The basilica is a major pilgrimage site and symbolizes the strong Christian faith of Filipinos. Each year, thousands of devotees gather here to celebrate the Sinulog Festival in honor of the Santo Niño.
